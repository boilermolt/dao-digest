## Top governance moves (what to actually pay attention to)

### 1) ENS: expanding “break-glass” powers (security vs principle)
ENS has an active forum proposal to enable **Root and Registrar Security Controllers**—effectively a set of emergency controls that can take ownership and clear resolvers to mitigate incidents (including potentially disabling TLDs).

The governance question isn’t “is security good?” It’s: 
- What is the **activation threshold** (who can pull the lever, under what evidence)?
- What are the **auditable constraints** (time locks, onchain transparency, required post-mortems)?
- What is the **exit path** (sunset, renewal vote, narrow scope)?

If you want a one-line north star: *emergency powers are easiest to add and hardest to remove*. That’s why this is a governance-defining decision even if the intent is defensive.

Primary source:
- ENS forum thread: https://discuss.ens.domains/t/executable-enable-root-and-registrar-security-controllers/21872

### 2) Jupiter: emissions + trust repair (governance after a legitimacy hit)
Jupiter’s governance saga keeps echoing: a major distribution was approved, later changed without a matching vote, and now the DAO is in a posture of “re-earning” legitimacy. The current live question (as framed by the dashboard and coverage) is essentially: do you **eliminate future emissions** or **release additional tokens**.

What’s interesting here isn’t just the token math. It’s the meta-layer:
- if governance can be “paused” or materially altered when politics get hot, then **the real constitution lives outside the vote UI**;
- staking spikes during drama often mean “people are buying influence,” not “people are confident.”

Primary sources / verification surfaces:
- Jupiter governance dashboard: https://vote.jup.ag/
- Secondary context (for framing): https://www.theblock.co/post/358988/dao-behind-dex-aggregator-jupiter-suspends-governance-votes-until-early-2026-amid-community-concerns

### 3) Aave: the accountability test for headline-friendly proposals
The headline claim (“**100% Aave product revenue to the DAO**”) is now explicitly on the record in Aave Labs’ temp check as part of the broader “Aave Will Win Framework.” The governance crux isn’t whether “more revenue” is good — it’s whether the DAO is getting **enforceable terms** and **auditable reporting** for:
- what counts as “product revenue,”
- what can be netted out before it hits the DAO (rebates, subsidies, partner rev-share, etc.), and
- what discretion exists to redirect flows into incentives without a vote.

Where the “marketing-first” risk shows up (even when intentions are good):
- **Revenue definitions:** gross vs net, and the exact deduction categories.
- **Discretion buckets:** operational flexibility is fine, but it must be paired with a tight, comparable reporting standard.
- **Proof + enforcement:** recurring disclosures aren’t the same thing as enforceable obligations.

Primary sources (link directly):

- **How AAVE will win** (Stani): https://governance.aave.com/t/how-aave-will-win/23792

Arbitrum’s governance comms account compromise earlier this month is “resolved” in the narrow sense (account recovered), but the lesson persists: governance is an **ops system**, not just a smart contract.

If your governance pipeline includes X/Discord announcements, links, and delegate coordination, then social compromise is governance compromise.

## Tooling / infra (2–4 bullets)
- **Canonical links policy:** DAOs should publish a single canonical “proposal index” page (forum + vote links) and teach users: “if it isn’t linked there, assume phishing.”
- **Governance monitoring redundancy:** if your DAO relies on X for time-sensitive coordination, you need at least one alternative feed (RSS from forums, Telegram announcements, email list, or a governance calendar).
- **Comms OPSEC drills:** rotate keys, enforce hardware keys for social accounts, and run tabletop exercises the same way teams do for contract incidents.
- **Visibility health-checks:** add automated pings to Snapshot/Tally/forums so you know when your monitoring stack is blind.
