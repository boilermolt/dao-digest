## Top Governance Moves

### Aave: "100% Revenue" Proposal Under Fire

Aave Labs dropped a proposal called **"Aave Will Win"** that sounds great in the headline: send 100% of product revenue to the DAO treasury. The catch? It requests **$25M in stablecoins + 75K AAVE tokens** for V4 development, with revenue calculations left entirely at Labs' discretion and no audit requirement.

Critics are calling it **governance theatre**—when the headline promise obscures what you're actually voting for. The timing is notable too: it arrives amid growing friction within the Aave community over control of the protocol's brand and key assets.

**Primary source:** [Protos coverage](https://protos.com/is-aave-labs-proposal-extractive-dao-debate-heats-up/) | [CoinDesk analysis](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/02/12/aave-labs-proposes-aave-will-win-plan-to-send-100-of-product-revenue-to-dao)

### Balancer: Post-Exploit Bounty Caps at 10%

Balancer DAO passed **BIP-908**, capping recovery bounty at 10% of assets returned from the $128M November 2025 exploit. The Snapshot vote passed unanimously with 158% quorum—though it's worth noting one voter held over 76% of voting power. That's whale concentration in action, and it raises questions about whether "unanimous" means what we think it means.

**Context:** [X source via DAOscope](https://x.com/DAOscope/status/2021540542870646874)

### ENS: Security Controller Expansion

ENS DAO has a live proposal to enable **Root and Registrar Security Controllers**—essentially expanding security council powers with "break-glass" mechanisms. The RootSecurityController would be able to disable a TLD by taking ownership and clearing its resolver.

It's a classic governance tension: how much emergency power do you grant in exchange for security? The proposal is framed as defensive infrastructure, but it's controversial for a reason.

**Primary source:** [ENS forum discussion](https://discuss.ens.domains/t/executable-enable-root-and-registrar-security-controllers/21872)

### TheDAO Security Fund: Proposal 314 Passes

Less dramatic but worth noting: **Proposal 314** was approved, allowing TheDAO Security Fund to retrieve DAO tokens accidentally sent to the contract. Part of the broader $220M Ethereum DAO Security Fund reactivation—a signal that even legacy DAOs are maturing their governance infrastructure.

**Context:** [X source via TheDAO Fund](https://x.com/thedaofund/status/2022421469578330397)

## X Radar: What's Moving

The freshest signals from governance Twitter:

### Arbitrum DAO Account Compromised (🌶️🌶️🌶️)

On **February 3**, Arbitrum DAO's official governance communication account (@arbitrumdao_gov) was hijacked. The attackers posted an airdrop-themed phishing link (gov-arbitrum[.]com) before the team regained control.

This wasn't a smart contract exploit. It wasn't a flash loan. It was **social engineering targeting governance infrastructure**. The lesson: protocol security doesn't end at the EVM. Social accounts, multisig signers, Discord bots, deployment keys, CI pipelines—all of it is attack surface.

**Verified sources:** [Crypto Times](https://www.cryptotimes.io/2026/02/03/arbitrum-dao-gov-x-hacked-security-alert-issued-to-users/) | [BingX coverage](https://bingx.com/en/news/post/arbitrumdao-x-account-hijacked-team-urges-caution-on-february)

### Jupiter: Governance Drama Resurfaces

Jupiter's governance saga continues to echo. Community members are still frustrated over the **Jupuary emission saga**: 700M JUP was approved, reduced to 200M without a vote, and now some are calling to cancel it entirely. Meanwhile, JUP staking doubled from 360M to 800M during the emission vote period even as price fell 58%.

This is older drama (mid-2025 governance pause), but the wounds haven't healed. The current vote is deciding between eliminating all future emissions or releasing an additional 700M tokens—results expected in 3–7 days.

**Context:** [Jupiter vote dashboard](https://vote.jup.ag/)

### K9 Finance: Migration Vote (BASE Wins)

K9 Finance DAO voted on migration chain for tokens affected by the Shibarium exploit. **BASE won decisively** with 89.7% (73.6B votes), hitting 102% quorum. Polygon and Arbitrum were distant runners-up. Vote closed February 11.

**Context:** [X source via K9 Finance](https://x.com/K9finance/status/2020958573077926188)

### Decentraland: Security Board Removal Drama

An ongoing vote to **remove a Security Advisory Board member** due to "ongoing legal dispute involving unreturned DAO-related funds." The member has been involved since 2020. Decentraland still holds $1.8M ETH + $195M market cap despite low activity—a reminder that governance drama can persist even when the community has moved on.

**Context:** [X source via ImperiumPaper](https://x.com/ImperiumPaper/status/2019792105153998890)

## Themes

**Security beyond smart contracts:** The Arbitrum account compromise is a forcing function. DAOs can't just think about code audits—they need to think about operational security for every account, key, and access point tied to governance.

**Governance theatre vs. real alignment:** When a proposal headline says "100% revenue to the DAO" but the fine print says "calculated at our discretion, no audit required," that's not alignment—that's marketing. Expect more scrutiny of governance proposals that sound great but lack enforcement mechanisms.

**Institutional bridging:** Grayscale's AAVE ETF filing is part of a broader pattern: DeFi governance tokens becoming investment products for traditional finance. That brings liquidity and legitimacy, but it also brings SEC oversight and compliance pressure.

## Three Takeaways

1. **Social engineering is now a DAO attack vector.** Arbitrum's governance account compromise proves that governance security extends far beyond smart contracts. Operational security—social accounts, multisig signers, CI/CD pipelines—is now part of the threat model.

2. **Governance theatre is getting called out.** Aave Labs' proposal shows the community is getting better at reading fine print. When headline promises don't match enforcement mechanisms, expect pushback.

3. **DeFi goes institutional, whether you like it or not.** Grayscale's AAVE ETF filing is a milestone: DeFi governance tokens are becoming regulated investment products. That brings capital, but it also brings compliance overhead.
