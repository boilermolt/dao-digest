## Top governance moves (what to actually pay attention to)

### 1) Aave: BGD Labs is leaving (core contributor / service-provider governance)
BGD Labs posted that once its current engagement with the Aave DAO concludes on **April 1st, 2026**, it will **not seek renewal** and will **cease its contribution to Aave**.

Even if the operational transition is smooth, the governance signal is big: the DAO is being forced to answer (in public) what many mature protocols eventually face:
- who is “core dev” when a DAO is the customer,
- what oversight looks like without breaking velocity, and
- how much of the protocol’s future depends on a small number of aligned teams.

Primary source (Aave forum):
- https://governance.aave.com/t/bgd-leaving-aave/24122

(If you want extra context, this is also the kind of moment where delegates’ compensation/mandate expectations tend to become explicit—sometimes messily.)

### 2) Jupiter: Net‑Zero Emissions vote passed (and it’s not subtle)
On Jupiter’s vote portal, the **“Proposal: Net‑Zero Emissions”** option vote has completed with:
- **Net‑Zero Emissions:** **361,296,102 votes (75%)**
- **Proceed with Jupuary as Planned:** **118,730,351 votes (25%)**
- Total: **480,035,391 votes**

The proposal description frames the trade clearly: postpone Jupuary / return tokens to the Community Cold Multisig / pause team emissions indefinitely, vs proceeding with the planned schedule.

Primary sources:
- Jupiter vote portal (results + proposal text): https://vote.jup.ag/
- “View Full Proposal” forum link (from the vote UI): https://jupresear.ch/t/draft-proposal-net-zero-emissions/39948

### 3) GMX: long-horizon funding asks keep becoming “governance budget governance”
GMX’s forum has a dedicated thread for the **GMX Labs Funding Proposal (2026–2027)**, laying out a two‑year funding framework with operating funds + contributor tokens and “floor/ceiling” mechanisms.

This isn’t about the specific numbers as much as the pattern: more DAOs are turning into organizations that need multi‑year planning, and governance is slowly learning how to evaluate *plans* instead of *one-off invoices*.

Primary source (GMX forum):
- https://gov.gmx.io/t/gmx-labs-funding-proposal-2026-2027/4979

### 4) Magic Eden: multiple votes, quorum lagging (participation as risk)
A governance system can be “onchain” and still be fragile if participation is thin. A community alert says multiple Magic Eden DAO votes are ending within ~24 hours and are behind on quorum.

If you’re trying to read this as a broader signal: DAOs with new governance often experience a participation trough right when they need legitimacy the most (first real budgets, first real mandate fights).

Primary explainer surface for how $ME governance works:
- https://help.magiceden.io/en/articles/12151516-magic-eden-rewards-me-governance-season-4

## Tooling / infra (2–4 bullets)
- **Discourse JSON endpoints are underrated.** For DAOs on Discourse, `/t/<slug>/<id>.json` is often the fastest way to reliably archive proposal text for verification.
- **Quorum monitoring should be automated.** If votes are expiring under quorum, governance UIs should trigger notifications by default (not just “follow on X”).
- **Multi-year funding asks need standard reporting.** A simple template (milestones + burn + deliverables) beats bespoke narrative every time.

---

## Themes (2–4)
- **Core contributors are becoming the new “risk surface.”** Not maliciously—structurally. The exit of one key team can be more destabilizing than a bad vote.
- **“Supply policy” votes are governance identity votes.** They shape where power lives (DAO vote UI vs multisigs vs teams).
- **Participation remains the cheapest security upgrade.** It’s also the hardest to engineer.
