## Top governance moves (2–4)
### 1) Optimism: “Align OP Token with Superchain Success” (buybacks)
**Venue:** Optimism forum (Season 9 proposal)
- **What it is:** A proposed **12‑month buyback program** using **50% of incoming Superchain revenue** (sequencer ETH contributed by OP Chains like **Base, Uniswap/Unichain, World Chain**, etc.).
- **How they plan to execute:** Monthly **OTC conversions** of ETH → OP, with a pre‑defined execution window and **no price discretion**. They explicitly say they’ll publish an **execution dashboard** (fills, pacing, pricing, balances).
- **Pauses/guardrails (per post):** Conversion pauses and rolls if (1) monthly revenue < **$200k** equivalent, (2) provider can’t execute under a pre‑agreed **max fee spread**, or (3) the window is missed.
- **Why it matters:** This is Optimism trying to thread the needle: keep OP as a governance token, but add a *structural demand loop* tied to Superchain throughput.
- **Status:** Forum proposal; updated **Jan 15, 2026** to split the original idea into **two proposals**.
- **Link:** https://gov.optimism.io/t/proposal-to-align-the-op-token-with-superchain-success/10527

### 2) ENS: EP 6.28 “ENS Retro” (onchain executable, queued)
**Venue:** Tally (ENS onchain governance)
- **What passed:** An **onchain executable** proposal funding a **mixed‑methods governance + spend retrospective** (stakeholder analysis → spend retro → synthesis & roadmap).
- **Budget mechanics:** **$125,000** requested to the **MetaGov Working Group multisig**; distribution described as milestone‑based with explicit **clawback/stop** language via social proposal.
- **Vote outcome (do not editorialize this part):** **Succeeded** with **For: 1.28M / Against: 573.29K / Abstain: 0.01**; quorum shown as **1M**.
- **Where it’s at now:** **Queued / Pending execution**.
- **Why it matters:** DAOs rarely fund “organizational self‑diagnosis” as a first‑class deliverable. This vote says ENS is willing to pay for legitimacy and accountability tooling — but it’s also the kind of work that can inflame politics if deliverables feel soft.
- **Link:** https://www.tally.xyz/gov/ens/proposal/55749099144243971799440687308450608384225109120486751685653209891316805274409

### 3) ENS: Snapshot temp check — Delegation Incentives Program (pilot)
**Venue:** Snapshot (via Tally Snapshot API)
- **What it proposes:** A **90‑day pilot** paying incentives to **active delegates** and their **delegators**, with monthly distributions and reporting.
- **Sizing:** Up to **90k ENS** authorized (with a variable monthly pool based on “active voting power increase” bands).
- **Design choices that jumped out:** time‑weighted balances (180‑day window), caps per delegate/delegator, and a **“<1 ENS payout → lottery”** mechanism to avoid gas‑inefficient dust.
- **Why it matters:** This is the “pay for participation” playbook getting sharper — not just bribing for votes, but paying for *sustained* delegation and activity.
- **Link (proposal list endpoint; proposal title [6.31]):** https://www.tally.xyz/api/proposals/snapshot/ens.eth?limit=50

## Down in the weeds (3–6)
- **ENS Retro deliverables are framed like a research project** (stakeholder interviews, document inventory, mixed methods). That can be a feature (rigor) or a governance smell (hard to measure “done”). The milestone review points are the control surface — watch how the MetaGov WG operationalizes them.
- **Optimism’s pause condition ($200k monthly revenue) is a quiet governance statement**: it avoids “performative buybacks” in thin months, but it also creates a predictable question every month (“did we clear the threshold?”) that can become political if the program is close to the line.
- **ENS delegation incentives pilot uses “active delegate” as a rolling threshold (7 of last 10 onchain votes)**. That’s a nice, crisp rule — but it will shape delegate behavior (no more skipping “boring” votes).
- **The incentives proposal explicitly aims to increase capture cost** (they cite a simulated jump in capture cost from ~$11M to ~$94M under an aggressive delegation scenario). That framing is good: it’s security, not vibes.
- **Incident-response governance is maturing into settlement governance** (Gyroscope as the template). The vote is often the easy part; the hard part is proving follow‑through (funds movement + published remediation plan).
