## Top governance moves (2–4)

### 1) Aave: “focus the multichain strategy” moves from philosophy to levers
Aave’s ARFC on pruning low-activity deployments is now less a vibe and more a checklist. The core action is straightforward: **freeze Aave V3 on zkSync, Metis, and Soneium** to reduce ongoing governance + risk overhead where there’s little organic traction.

What makes it bigger than a cleanup PR is the forward policy: the ARFC proposes that **new deployments should come with a $2M/year revenue floor** guaranteed by the chain ecosystem (“if you want Aave, help pay for the ongoing governance/security tax”). That’s a subtle but real shift in how “expansion” is justified.

- Primary source (forum thread + update): https://governance.aave.com/t/arfc-focussing-the-aave-v3-multichain-strategy-phase-1/23954

### 2) Arbitrum: DVP-based quorum (floor/ceiling) is a governance design upgrade
Arbitrum is proposing to define quorum as:

> **Quorum = min{max quorum, max{α * DVP, baseline quorum}}**

Translation: quorum scales with *actual delegated voting power*, but it never drops below a baseline and never climbs above a cap.

The details matter here because they’re putting hard parameters on the social contract:
- **Constitutional**: α = 0.5, baseline = 150M ARB, max = 450M ARB
- **Non-constitutional**: α = 0.4, baseline = 100M ARB, max = 300M ARB

They also link an audit (Trail of Bits) and flag a practical gotcha: the onchain upgrade needs an initial DVP estimate, and governance can later correct it if the estimate is off.

- Primary source (forum): https://forum.arbitrum.foundation/t/constitutional-dvp-quorum-for-arbitrumdao-implementation-parameters/30484

### 3) Optimism: buybacks as “token purpose,” with execution details as the real test
Optimism’s proposal to align OP with Superchain success is explicit: **50% of incoming Superchain revenue** gets converted into OP via **monthly OTC** conversions for **12 months** (starting February), with the purchased OP held in the Collective treasury.

The credibility hinge isn’t the narrative; it’s the instrumentation:
- promised **execution dashboard** (fills, pricing, balances)
- a pre-defined window each month
- guardrails to pause/roll conversion if revenue is too low or the OTC spread is unacceptable

The governance design question to watch is whether “no discretion” stays true once real market conditions show up.

- Primary source (forum): https://gov.optimism.io/t/proposal-to-align-the-op-token-with-superchain-success/10527

### 4) ENS: treasury ops proposal to unjam execution (EP 6.32)
This one is a reminder that DAOs don’t fail only by bad votes; they fail by **cash management**.

The ENS thread argues that a previously approved Working Group funding execution can’t fully execute because the ENS DAO timelock doesn’t have enough USDC (with context pointing to Superfluid AutoWrap moving USDC into streaming mechanics). The fix on the table: a **one-time 2.5M USDC transfer** from the Endowment Safe to the timelock (`wallet.ensdao.eth`).

- Primary source (forum): https://discuss.ens.domains/t/ep-6-32-executable-transfer-2-5m-usdc-from-endowment-to-wallet-ensdao-eth/21882
