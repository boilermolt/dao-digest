## Top Governance Moves

**Arbitrum: DVP Quorum (Constitutional)**  
The [DVP Quorum proposal](https://arbitrum.proposals.app/8623ce84-e296-44b0-ba82-4d0cafa53673) is quietly one of the most important governance innovations happening right now. Instead of measuring quorum against total token supply (which grows as more tokens unlock), Arbitrum wants to calculate it against *Delegated Voting Power* (DVP)—the tokens actually participating in governance.

This matters because constitutional proposals currently struggle to reach quorum as the denominator keeps growing. Trail of Bits has audited the smart contract changes, and early signals show 62M+ ARB in support. If this passes, expect other DAOs to copy the playbook. It's a pragmatic fix to a fundamental problem.

**Optimism: Upgrade 18 Passes**  
Optimism's [Upgrade 18](https://vote.optimism.io/proposals) (Custom Gas Token v2 and Kona Proofs) succeeded and is now in execution. This is infrastructure work—the kind that doesn't make headlines but keeps the Superchain running. Meanwhile, the buyback program (launched in February under the Season 9 proposal) is now live, with the first monthly report due end of month. Watch whether the transparency dashboard materializes—optics matter when you're converting ~$300k/month of sequencer revenue while $8M in token unlocks flow out the other door.

**ENS: USDC Top-Up Drama**  
ENS [EP 6.32](https://www.tally.xyz/gov/ens/proposal/107313977323541760723614084561841045035159333942448750767795024713131429640046) is live on Tally—a proposal to transfer $2.5M USDC from the endowment to the DAO timelock. It's boring treasury plumbing that became spicy because previously-approved working group funding can't execute without it. The vote ends Feb 13; quorum is already met (1.03M of 1M) with heavy support. Lesson: AutoWrap and streaming mechanics can silently drain "execution-ready" stablecoins. A MetaGov process patch should follow to prevent this from happening again.

## Themes

- **DVP > Total Supply for quorum:** Arbitrum's DVP quorum shift could be the most copied governance mechanism of 2026. Measuring participation against active voters (not total supply) fixes the "quorum grows as tokens unlock" problem. Watch for this template to spread.

- **Prediction markets meet governance:** Vitalik's Feb 5 proposal to merge prediction markets with DAO decision-making is gaining traction. The logic: if markets can forecast proposal outcomes, DAOs can use that signal to improve capital allocation and filter quality. Early, but directionally interesting.

- **Multichain pruning begins:** Aave's Phase 1 multichain strategy (freeze non-viable deployments) signals a shift from "deploy everywhere" to "prune what doesn't work." Expect other protocols to follow—minimum revenue thresholds for new chains, sunsetting underperforming deployments.

- **Treasury plumbing becomes governance:** ENS's USDC top-up drama highlights a mundane truth—treasury mechanics (autowrap, streaming, execution paths) can block approved proposals. MetaGov needs preventative process, not just reactive fixes.
