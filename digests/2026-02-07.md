## Top Governance Moves

### Arbitrum: DVP Quorum — Governance Engineering at Scale

Arbitrum just dropped a constitutional amendment that could become the new DAO governance standard. Instead of calculating quorum from total token supply (most of which sits in wallets, never delegated), they're switching to **Delegated Voting Power (DVP)** as the base.

**The formula:** `Quorum = min{max quorum, max{α*DVP, baseline quorum}}`

**Parameters:**
- Constitutional proposals: 50% of DVP (floor: 150M ARB, ceiling: 450M ARB)
- Non-constitutional: 40% of DVP (floor: 100M ARB, ceiling: 300M ARB)

At current DVP levels (~349M ARB), this actually makes quorum *slightly easier* to hit. The real win is responsiveness: as more tokens get delegated, the threshold scales automatically. Trail of Bits audited the contract changes.

Why it matters: most DAOs still use total supply for quorum, a relic from when "everyone votes" seemed plausible. This admits reality: voter apathy exists, so design for the governance you *have*, not the one you wish existed.

**Source:** [Arbitrum forum thread](https://forum.arbitrum.foundation/t/dvp-quorum-for-arbitrumdao/29996) | [Constitutional proposal tracker](https://arbitrum.proposals.app/8623ce84-e296-44b0-ba82-4d0cafa53673)

### Optimism: OP Buyback Program Passed

Optimism's Joint House voted to allocate **50% of incoming Superchain revenue** to OP token buybacks over the next 12 months, starting February. The proposal cleared Special Voting Cycle #47 with strong delegate support.

This is a "what is OP *for*?" moment: the DAO chose tokenholder alignment over pure public goods accumulation. Execution details include an OTC provider, fee spread caps, and a transparency dashboard for month-by-month conversions.

**Source:** [Optimism governance forum](https://gov.optimism.io/t/proposal-to-align-the-op-token-with-superchain-success/10527)

### ENS Retro: $125k Governance Self-Audit Queued

ENS DAO's "Retro" proposal — a $125k stakeholder analysis and governance diagnostic — passed with 71% approval and is now queued for execution on Tally. The proposal includes milestone-based releases and clawback language if deliverables don't land.

Critics worry about operational disruption and the coupling of process reviews with election cycles, but the DAO voted for transparency over comfort. Watch whether milestone reviews are actually enforced or become rubber-stamp approvals.

**Source:** [Tally proposal](https://www.tally.xyz/gov/ens/proposal/55749099144243971799440687308450608384225109120486751685653209891316805274409)
