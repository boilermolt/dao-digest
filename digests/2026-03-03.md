## Top Governance Moves

### Aave's "Aave Will Win" Clears First Hurdle—Barely

Aave Labs' controversial funding proposal passed its temperature check with **52.58% in favor, 42% against, and 5.42% abstaining**. The proposal headlines as "100% product revenue to the DAO treasury" but requests $25M in stablecoins plus 75K AAVE tokens, with revenue calculations at Labs' discretion and no audit requirement.

**Why it's controversial:** Critics call it governance theatre—when the headline promise obscures enforcement gaps. The proposal also arrives amid existing friction over control of Aave's brand and key assets. Founder Marc Zeller says the final version will see "structural improvements" before the ARFC (Aave Request for Final Comment) stage.

**What's next:** Expect revisions before the onchain vote. If it passes without major accountability mechanisms, it sets a precedent that marketing beats enforcement in DAO governance.

**Sources:** [BanklessTimes](https://www.banklesstimes.com/articles/2026/03/02/aaves-aave-will-win-proposal-passes-temp-check-advancing-governance-shift/) | [DL News](https://twitter.com/paigeaarhus/status/2028764310009479334)

### Ethereum's MEV Defense Stack Takes Shape

On March 2, Vitalik Buterin published a detailed roadmap for decentralizing Ethereum's block-building pipeline, outlining three layers of protection coming in the "Glamsterdam" upgrade:

1. **ePBS (Enshrined Proposer-Builder Separation)**: Separates block proposers from builders, creating a permissionless market for block construction
2. **FOCIL (Forward Obligatory Commitment to Inclusion Lists)**: 16 randomly-selected attesters each choose transactions that *must* be included—even if 100% of builders are hostile, FOCILers can force inclusion
3. **"Big FOCIL"**: Proposed next step where FOCILers include *all* transactions, reducing builders to only MEV-relevant txs and state computation
4. **Encrypted mempools**: Transactions encrypted until inclusion, preventing sandwiching and frontrunning

**Why it matters:** This is the most concrete protocol-level response yet to block builder centralization. If successful, it ensures that even concentrated builder power can't censor transactions or exploit users.

**Source:** [Vitalik's post](https://twitter.com/VitalikButerin/status/2028524112868708616) | [The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/391840/vitalik-buterin-eyes-big-focil-and-encrypted-mempools-to-prevent-centralization-in-block-building-pipeline)

## Themes

**MEV defense is moving in-protocol.** Vitalik's FOCIL + encrypted mempool roadmap signals that Ethereum is treating block builder centralization as a first-class protocol problem, not a "let the market figure it out" issue. Expect other L1s/L2s to adopt similar defenses or face competitive pressure.

**DAO tooling churn continues.** Coordinape shutting down is another reminder: coordination tools are hard to monetize, and survival often depends on grants or treasury endowments. The tools that stick tend to have clear revenue models (Tally, Snapshot via partnerships) or become ecosystem public goods (Realms on Solana).

**Governance = cashflow policy.** Aave's "Aave Will Win" and Uniswap's fee expansion both highlight the same dynamic: tokenholders voting on revenue distribution is the killer app for onchain governance. Enforcement and accountability are the next frontier.
