## Top Governance Moves

**BlackRock + Uniswap = Institutional DeFi Awakening**  
BlackRock's $2.2B tokenized Treasury fund (BUIDL) is now tradable on Uniswap, marking the asset manager's first real DeFi move. BlackRock also bought UNI governance tokens—a signal that they're not just testing infrastructure but positioning for protocol influence. UNI jumped 25% on the news. This is the kind of "TradFi meets onchain" moment that redefines legitimacy for DeFi.

**Aave's Conflict-of-Interest Slugfest**  
ACI proposed a new [conflict-of-interest policy](https://governance.aave.com/t/arfc-addendum-mandatory-disclosures-and-conflict-of-interest-voting-norms/23995) requiring recipients of DAO funding to disclose wallet addresses and abstain from conflicted votes. The enforcement mechanism: conflicted votes would be "invalid for legitimacy purposes" and excluded from tallies—even if it changes the outcome. Aave Labs employees pushed back hard, calling it a "parallel governance system with no rules, no finality, and no clear authority." Stani Kulechov weighed in, calling the proposal "poorly written" and voting no. ACI head Marc Zeller framed it as a "quick patch" to mitigate the "slow-motion coup" the protocol is experiencing. Snapshot vote is live; as of mid-morning Feb 12, "aye" votes narrowly leading 489k to 485k. This is a proxy war over who really controls Aave.

**Arbitrum's Constitutional Upgrade: DVP Quorum**  
Arbitrum's [constitutional proposal](https://arbitrum.proposals.app/8623ce84-e296-44b0-ba82-4d0cafa53673) to shift quorum from total supply to Delegated Voting Power (DVP) is live and gaining momentum. Trail of Bits audited the smart contract changes; X signals show 62M+ ARB in early support. If this passes, it could become the most copied governance mechanism of 2026—solving the chronic "quorum is impossible because most tokens are dormant" problem. Watch for other DAOs to fork the model.

**ENS Abandons Namechain L2**  
ENS Labs [scrapped its Namechain L2 plan](https://www.theblock.co/post/388932/ens-labs-scraps-namechain-l2-shifts-ensv2-fully-ethereum-mainnet) and is deploying ENSv2 entirely on Ethereum mainnet. Reason: gas costs dropped so dramatically that a separate L2 no longer makes economic sense. Mainnet scaled faster than expected. ENS says the L2 architecture work wasn't wasted—they'll use it to improve interoperability with other L2s.

## 3 Takeaways

1. **BlackRock + Uniswap is the "DeFi goes institutional" moment.** When the world's largest asset manager buys governance tokens and makes their fund tradable onchain, the game changes. DAOs need to prepare for institutional voting blocs.

2. **Conflict-of-interest rules are the new governance frontier.** Aave's COI fight is messy, but it's asking the right question: how do you prevent governance capture without creating a subjective, unenforceable parallel system? Every DAO will face this.

3. **Ethereum mainnet won (for now).** ENS scrapping Namechain L2 because mainnet gas is cheap enough is a huge signal. If L1 keeps scaling, the "you need your own L2" narrative weakens—and DAOs can stay where liquidity already lives.
