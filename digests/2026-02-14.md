## Top Governance Moves

### Aave: "Will Win" Framework Under Fire

Aave Labs dropped a 4,000-word "temperature check" on Feb 12 titled **"Aave Will Win Framework"**—promising 100% of product revenue to the DAO treasury and a foundation to steward the Aave brand. Sounds like a peace offering after months of civil war over IP ownership and conflict-of-interest policies.

**But the devil's in the details.**

Marc Zeller, vocal DAO delegate and head of the Aave Chan Initiative (ACI), initially called the proposal **"extractive"** and a **"gaslight,"** accusing Labs of "raiding" DAO tokens "for zero actual enforceable commitment." His follow-up post acknowledged "victory" for the DAO but warned the proposal is **"four proposals in a trenchcoat"** with major red flags:

- **$50M token request = 31.5% of entire treasury** ($25M stables + 75,000 AAVE worth ~$25M) for a single service provider in a single vote
- **Revenue deductions at Labs' "sole discretion"** — no independent audit, no cap, no DAO approval threshold
- **75,000 AAVE would increase Labs' voting power** at a time when they're already accused of using undisclosed wallets to swing sensitive votes (see: the narrowly-rejected conflict-of-interest disclosure vote that ended yesterday)

Labs wants $5M stablecoins upfront, the rest streamed over 12 months, and AAVE unlocking linearly over two years. Additional $17.5M grants tied to "specific product launches" (TBD).

**The optics are rough:** In December, Labs triggered a "disgraceful" holiday vote against contributor Ernesto Boado's proposal, voted it down 55%-45% while most delegates abstained. Handing them another 75,000 AAVE tokens after that move? Governance hawks are circling.

AAVE is up ~2% on the news despite broader market weakness, but the community is far from consensus.

### BlackRock Enters the DeFi Boardroom (Again)

On Feb 11, BlackRock made its boldest DeFi move yet: **BUIDL** (its $2.2B tokenized Treasury fund) is now tradable on Uniswap via UniswapX, facilitated by Securitize. But the bigger story: **BlackRock acquired UNI governance tokens.** Amount undisclosed, but this is the first DeFi governance token on BlackRock's balance sheet.

UNI surged 25% on the news, then faded back. The market's still processing whether this is validation or a governance takeover preview.

**Why it matters:** TradFi isn't just using DeFi infrastructure—they're positioning for protocol influence. Uniswap DAO hasn't addressed institutional voting power concentration yet. If BlackRock's UNI holdings become public and they start voting, expect governance fireworks. And they won't be alone—Fidelity, State Street, and others are watching closely.
