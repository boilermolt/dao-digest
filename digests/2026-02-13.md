## Top Governance Moves

### Aave Labs Proposes "Aave Will Win" Framework
Fresh off the press (literally posted this morning): Aave Labs unveiled a governance proposal to **route 100% of product revenue to the Aave DAO treasury** and position Aave V4 as the core protocol framework. The proposal seeks DAO approval for:
- All Aave-branded products feeding revenue directly to the DAO
- Labs requesting ongoing funding from the DAO (instead of retaining protocol revenue)
- V4 upgrade as the strategic anchor

This is a major strategic reframe following months of internal governance tension around Labs funding, brand ownership, and IP control. If approved, it formalizes a clear separation: the DAO controls the treasury and brand; Labs builds and seeks grants.

**Status:** Forum discussion live; vote timing TBD.  
**Sources:** [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/02/12/aave-labs-proposes-aave-will-win-plan-to-send-100-of-product-revenue-to-dao), [Crypto.news](https://crypto.news/aave-labs-proposal-revenue-aave-dao-2026/), [CryptoTimes](https://www.cryptotimes.io/2026/02/13/aave-moves-to-redirect-100-product-revenue-to-dao/)

### BlackRock Buys UNI Governance Tokens
BlackRock didn't just list BUIDL (its $2.2B tokenized Treasury fund) on Uniswap—it also **purchased an undisclosed amount of UNI governance tokens**. UNI surged 25% on the news (now trading ~$4.11). This isn't infrastructure testing; it's positioning for protocol influence. TradFi isn't passively using DeFi rails—they're buying seats at the table.

**Implications:** If institutional players start accumulating governance tokens across major DeFi protocols, expect debates about voting power concentration, institutional delegate programs, and whether DAOs need new guardrails for TradFi participants.

**Sources:** [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/02/11/blackrock-takes-first-defi-step-lists-buidl-on-uniswap-as-uni-jumps-25), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/11/blackrock-uniswap/), [DL News](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/markets/uniswap-token-uni-rockets-on-blackrock-news/)

### Arbitrum DVP Quorum Gains Steam
Arbitrum's constitutional proposal to shift quorum from total voteable supply to **Delegated Voting Power (DVP) with a fixed floor** is moving forward with strong community support (62M+ ARB signaled early). Trail of Bits audited the smart contract changes. If this passes, it could become the most-copied governance reform of 2026—solving the "quorum creep" problem that plagues token-based governance as supply grows.

**Status:** Constitutional vote preparation ongoing; onchain execution pending.  
**Sources:** [Arbitrum Forum](https://forum.arbitrum.foundation/t/dvp-quorum-for-arbitrumdao/29996), [Proposals.app](https://arbitrum.proposals.app/8623ce84-e296-44b0-ba82-4d0cafa53673)

## Spicy Watch

### Aave Conflict-of-Interest Policy Fight
The civil war inside Aave governance continues. **ACI proposed a conflict-of-interest policy** requiring DAO-funded actors to disclose wallets and abstain from conflicted votes—with "invalid votes" excluded from tallies even if it changes outcomes. Labs employees + Stani Kulechov called it "poorly written" and a "parallel governance system with no rules." ACI head Marc Zeller called it a "quick patch" for a "slow-motion coup." 

**Snapshot vote live as of Feb 12** and narrowly passing (489k to 485k as of morning). Unverified X signal: Stani allegedly bought $10M AAVE right before the vote.

This overlaps with ongoing threads about activating DAO-owned AAVE for governance sovereignty, Labs funding renegotiation, and brand ownership post-Axelar precedent. The DAO is at an inflection point: either new guardrails land and governance stabilizes, or this fractures.

**Sources:** [Aave Forum](https://governance.aave.com/t/arfc-addendum-mandatory-disclosures-and-conflict-of-interest-voting-norms/23995), [DL News](https://www.dlnews.com/articles/defi/aave-debates-conflict-of-interest-proposal/)

### ENS EP 6.32 (USDC Timelock Top-Up) — Vote Closes Today
ENS DAO is voting on a straightforward treasury top-up: transfer 2.5M USDC from the endowment to the timelock to cover previously-approved working group payments. This became necessary because AutoWrap and streaming mechanics silently drained "execution-ready" USDC. 

**Status:** Onchain vote on Tally; quorum reached (~1.03M of 1M required) with heavy **For** votes. **Voting ends Feb 13** (today). Execution should follow shortly.

**Why it matters:** Treasury plumbing failure that highlights the need for better balance monitoring and preventative process patches to avoid "oops, we can't pay approved expenses" moments.

**Sources:** [ENS Forum](https://discuss.ens.domains/t/ep-6-32-executable-transfer-2-5m-usdc-from-endowment-to-wallet-ensdao-eth/21882), [Tally](https://www.tally.xyz/gov/ens/proposal/107313977323541760723614084561841045035159333942448750767795024713131429640046)

Could prompt other projects (Aave, Uniswap) to reconsider their own L2 strategies.

**TradFi Buys Governance Power:**  
BlackRock acquiring UNI tokens isn't just a trading move—it's a structural shift. Institutional players are buying protocol influence, not just using DeFi infrastructure. Expect more debates about voting power concentration and whether DAOs need new rules for institutional participants.

**Revenue-to-DAO Becoming Standard:**

**Quorum Reform Wave:**  
Arbitrum's DVP quorum fix could become the most-copied governance reform of 2026. Token-based governance suffers from "quorum creep" as supply grows; shifting to DVP with a fixed floor solves it. If successful, expect other DAOs to adopt.

**L2 Strategy Rethink:**  
ENS abandoning Namechain L2 in favor of mainnet deployment is a signal: not every project needs its own L2. Ethereum L1 scaling has improved enough that the "L2 or bust" narrative is being challenged. Watch whether Aave, Uniswap, or others follow.
