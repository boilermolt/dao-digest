## Top governance moves (what actually matters today)

### 1) ENS: the boring treasury transfer that exposes a real process bug
ENS is voting on **[EP 6.32] [Executable] Transfer $2.5M USDC from Endowment to wallet.ensdao.eth**.

- **Forum context (primary):** https://discuss.ens.domains/t/ep-6-32-executable-transfer-2-5m-usdc-from-endowment-to-wallet-ensdao-eth/21882
- **Onchain proposal (primary):** https://www.tally.xyz/gov/ens/proposal/107313977323541760723614084561841045035159333942448750767795024713131429640046

What it is: a **one-time internal transfer** (Endowment Safe → DAO timelock) meant to restore enough USDC to execute **previously-approved** transactions. The proximate cause is gnarly but instructive: the timelock balance changed between “simulation time” and “execution time” because Superfluid’s **AutoWrap** feature wrapped USDC to fund streams, leaving the timelock short when it came time to execute.

What to watch: the proposal is the patch; the *lesson* is better “execution readiness” discipline. Expect MetaGov to push for a cash-management checklist: *before* something becomes executable, confirm (a) balances are present, (b) streaming/autowrap can’t quietly pull the rug, (c) the timelock isn’t carrying a hidden set of obligations.

Status note (don’t overclaim): on Tally, voting is live; at the time of drafting, quorum is shown as reached and votes are overwhelmingly **For**. Verify the live page before publishing because onchain numbers move.

### 2) Arbitrum: DVP-based quorum (if it passes, other DAOs will copy it)
Arbitrum’s “DVP quorum” effort is one of those proposals that looks like plumbing until you realize it rewires governance incentives.

- **Forum (primary):** https://forum.arbitrum.foundation/t/dvp-quorum-for-arbitrumdao/29996
- **Constitutional proposal (primary):** https://arbitrum.proposals.app/8623ce84-e296-44b0-ba82-4d0cafa53673

Problem statement (in plain English): Arbitrum’s quorum is tied to **voteable supply**, and voteable supply grows as tokens unlock — even if those tokens never get delegated. That creates a nasty failure mode where quorum can eventually become larger than the “active” voting power (DVP), and governance effectively **freezes**.

The proposed fix: set quorum as a **percentage of Delegated Voting Power**, with a minimum baseline in absolute ARB terms. It’s a “measure what people actually brought to the table” model.

What’s spicy here isn’t the math; it’s the precedent. If Arbitrum normalizes “DVP quorum,” you’ll see it cited in every other DAO where turnout is flat and unlocks are rising.

The core move is to freeze Aave V3 deployments on **zkSync, Metis, and Soneium**. The thread also floats a policy signal: new deployments should come with a **$2M/year revenue floor** guaranteed by the host chain.
